## 摘要
精确的机器学习原子间势函数（MLIP）使得第一性原理质量的势能面越来越易于用于凝聚相化学，但其推理成本仍可能限制为计算实验相关可观测量所需的采样。本工作结合迁移学习和知识蒸馏，构建紧凑的“学生”模型，这些模型保留了通过迁移学习应用于基础模型所获得的大得多的“教师”模型的精度。所得学生模型将实际模拟成本降低约一个数量级，使高精度采样对于挑战性的凝聚相问题变得切实可行。我们在三个采样复杂性递增的问题上演示了这一点：冰Ih的有限温度NPT模拟、240-370 K范围内液态水的经典和路径积分模拟，以及锐钛矿TiO2(101)/水界面处水分子解离的路径积分伞形采样模拟。在所有情况下，蒸馏得到的学生模型比直接在有限参考数据上训练的同等大小模型更可靠地重现其教师模型的目标可观测量。从Δ学习得到的CCSD(T)质量教师模型蒸馏的液态水学生模型，在整个研究温度范围内重现了热力学、结构、输运和核量子性质。在TiO2/水界面，蒸馏使PIMD伞形采样变得可行，并表明核量子效应使解离势垒降低约2 kcal/mol，并将分子-解离自由能差移向与最近固态17O NMR测量定量一致的值。我们的工作展示了知识蒸馏如何使精确的MLIP适用于将凝聚相反应热力学与实验联系起来的采样方法，尤其是对界面化学和催化而言。

## Abstract
Accurate machine learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) have made first-principles-quality potential energy surfaces increasingly accessible for condensed-phase chemistry, but their inference cost can still limit the sampling needed to compute experimentally relevant observables. In this work, we combine transfer learning and knowledge distillation to construct compact "student" models that retain the accuracy of much larger "teacher" models obtained by applying transfer learning to foundation models. The resulting students reduce production simulation cost by roughly an order of magnitude, making high-accuracy sampling practical for challenging condensed-phase problems. We demonstrate this across three problems of increasing sampling complexity: finite-temperature NPT simulations of ice Ih, classical and path-integral simulations of liquid water over 240-370 K, and path-integral umbrella-sampling simulations of water dissociation at the anatase TiO2(101)/water interface. In all cases, the distilled students reproduce the target observables of their teachers more reliably than models of the same size trained directly on the limited reference data. The liquid-water student, distilled from a Δ-learned CCSD(T)-quality teacher, reproduces thermodynamic, structural, transport, and nuclear quantum properties over the full temperature range studied. At the TiO2/water interface, distillation makes PIMD umbrella sampling practical and shows that nuclear quantum effects lower the dissociation barrier by roughly 2 kcal/mol and shift the molecular-dissociated free energy difference into quantitative agreement with recent solid-state 17O NMR measurements. Our work demonstrates how knowledge distillation can make accurate MLIPs practical for the sampling methods needed to connect condensed-phase reaction thermodynamics with experiment, notably for interfacial chemistry and catalysis.
